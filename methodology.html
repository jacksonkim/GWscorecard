Overview of Scoring Framework

Georgia Watch’s Hospital Transparency Scorecard measures hospital performance across four main categories. Each category contributes to an overall score reflecting transparency, affordability, and social responsibility.

Evaluation Categories

Each hospital is evaluated across four key categories. Click a card to explore the focus of each area.

Financial Transparency & Institutional Health

Measures how openly hospitals share financial data and maintain fiscal stability, transparency, and staff investment.

Community Benefit Spending

Evaluates how hospitals invest in their communities relative to their tax-exempt benefits and strategic outreach programs.

Healthcare Affordability & Billing

Assesses billing practices, financial assistance availability, and affordability for patients of varying income levels.

Healthcare Access & Social Responsibility

Examines service range, workforce equity, demographic alignment, and community access to essential health resources.

Data Sources

Our analysis draws on publicly available data verified through multiple independent and governmental sources.

Frequently Asked Questions

How are grades converted to stars?

Letter grades (A–F) are mapped to a five-star scale for accessibility. Half-stars represent intermediate values (e.g., B+ = 4.5 stars).

Why are some hospitals missing data?

Data availability varies based on reporting requirements and public disclosures. Missing data is treated neutrally.

How often is the scorecard updated?

The scorecard is updated annually or when new public datasets become available, ensuring transparency and accuracy.
